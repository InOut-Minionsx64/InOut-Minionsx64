Humayun's tomb is the tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun in

 Delhi, India.The tomb was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in

 1993,  and since then has undergone extensive restoration work,

 which is complete. Besides the main tomb enclosure of Humayun,

 several smaller monuments dot the pathway leading up to it, from

                                         the main entrance in the West, including one that even pre-dates the

                                         main tomb itself, by twenty years; it is the tomb complex of Isa

                                         Khan Niyazi, an Afghan noble in Sher Shah Suri's court of the Suri

                                         dynasty, who fought against the Mughals, constructed in 1547 CE.
